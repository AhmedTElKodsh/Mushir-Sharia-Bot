Is investing in a company with 5% haram revenue permissible?
Composing... based on AAOIFI FAS-21
● COMPLIANT
Based on AAOIFI FAS-21 (Section 4/3), investment in a company whose primary activity is permissible is acceptable when incidental non-compliant revenue does not exceed 5% [1]. The company's halal food production qualifies as a permissible primary activity, and 5% incidental haram revenue falls within the impurity tolerance threshold.
📖 FAS-21 §4/3 [1]
AAOIFI FAS-21, Section 4/3:
"It is permissible to invest in companies whose primary activity is permissible even if a small portion of their income is derived from impermissible sources, provided that the impermissible income does not exceed 5% of the company's total revenue."

Is a conventional bank loan with riba permissible?
● NON-COMPLIANT
Conventional loans involving riba (interest) are not permissible under AAOIFI standards. FAS-2 explicitly prohibits any transaction structured around predetermined interest. [FAS-2 §3/1]

What about a murabahah with late payment penalty?
● PARTIALLY COMPLIANT
The murabahah structure itself is permissible under FAS-2. However, charging additional fees specifically for late payment may constitute a penalty that requires careful structuring to avoid resembling riba. [FAS-2 §5/2]

What are the AAOIFI requirements for a sukuk issuance?
● COMPLIANT
Under AAOIFI FAS-17, sukuk must represent ownership in real assets or usufruct, with returns tied to asset performance — not a fixed return. The SPV must be bankruptcy-remote and assets must be Sharia-compliant. [FAS-17 §3/1]
