 Preface  
PR1 In the recent years, c ertain Islamic financial institutions (IFIs / the institutions) have initiated offering 
certain additional benefits, such as promotional gifts or prizes, to customers  including, in particular , 
those  who hold investment accounts (or at times other types of deposits) with the institution. This 
practice is particularly common in specific regions , where the routine returns on investment 
accounts were observed on a lower side, and where the conventional financial institutions have also 
been offering similar incentives for maintaining deposits with them . In addition, certain Sukuk 
programs have been introduced involving a similar structure.  
PR2 As such promotional g ifts and prizes awarded by the institutions are on a voluntary basis , and have 
certain significant Shari’ah compliance risks, AAOIFI had earlier issued a Shari’ah standard on this 
subject1. This standard aims to address the challenges on accounting  and financial reporting side of 
such promotional gifts and prizes.

 Introduction  
Overview  
IN1 This standard prescribes accounting and financial reporting principles for recognition, measurement, 
presentation and disclosures applicable  to promotional gifts and prizes awarded by the Islamic 
financial institutions (IFIs / the institutions) to their customers, including quasi -equity and other 
investment accountholders . 
Rationale for issuing  this standard  
IN2 During the revision process of AAOIFI Financial Accounting Standard ( FAS) 27 “Investment 
Accounts” , it became evident that certain  institutions have  initiated offering certain additional 
benefits, such as promotional gifts or prizes, to the investment accountholders (and at times , to 
their stakeholders) . 
  
IN3 The AAOIFI Accounting Board ( AAB / the board ) noted that there is a lack of guidance on the 
accounting and financial reporting aspects  of promotional gifts and prizes , and a ccordingly, decided 
to develop a specific FAS on this subject.   
 
IN4 While issuing this standard, AAOIFI , in no manner whatsoever, intends to encourage  the practice of 
offering promotional prizes .

AAOIFI Financial Accounting Standard  (FAS)  48  
Promotional Gifts and Prizes  
Objective of the standard  
1. The objective of this standard is to prescribe the accounting and financial reporting requirements 
applicable to  promotional  gifts and prizes  awarded  by the Islamic financial institutions ( IFIs / the 
institutions) to their customers , including quasi -equity and other investment accountholders . 
Scope  
2. This standard shall apply  to promotional gifts and prizes (including loyalty programs) announced and 
awarded by an institution (on behalf of the owners  and out of the amounts attributable to the 
owners’ equity ) to all or specific customers including quasi -equity and other investment 
accountholders . This standard shall be read in conjunction with AAOIFI Shari’ah Standard ( SS) 55 
“Competitions and Prizes”, which provides the Shari’ah principles and rules under which such 
programs and their respective types can be offered.  
3. This standard shall not apply to Hiba provided by the institution on behalf of the owners  as a profit 
smoothening technique , during the computation of periodic profits and losses of an investment pool 
at the time of constructive liquidation , that falls within the scope of respective AAOIFI FAS2. 
Definition  
4. For the purpose of  interpreting and applying this standard, the following short definitions are 
relevant:  
a. Promotional prize – in the specific context of this standard, is a prize  (in the form of 
monetary or non -monetary reward)  used to promote specific products or services or to 
attract invest ments  by way of holding a draw  or by achieving a defined target , or a 
combination of both  which is awarded at a given point in time in future ;  
b. Promotional gift  – in the specific context of this standard, is a gift used to promote specific 
products or services or to attract investments that is awarded  by an institution , at its sole 
discretion,  from time to time to certain  customers ; 
c. Loyalty program  – is usually in the form of points accumulated by the customers against the 
purchase or continued utilisation of products or services3, or for maintaining investment s, 
enabling the customers to exchange accumulated points for cash, goods or services on a 
complementary basis or at discounted rates  at a future date.

 Types of entitlement  
5. Promotional gifts and prizes are categori sed into the following types:  
a. promotional gifts  where entitlement occurs instantly at a specific point in time;  
b. promotional prizes that are announced in advance to be awarded at a future date, mostly 
contingent on a draw or achievement of a target ; and 
c. loyalty program s where obligation is accumulated over a period of time . 
Recognition and measurement  
Promotional gifts  
6. An institution shall record an expense with regard to promotional gifts, as and when incurred.  
Promotional prizes  
Initial recognition  
7. An institution shall record a provision against  the obligation arising from  promotional prizes , as and 
when:  
a. there is a n existing constructive obligation requiring the institution to award the 
promotional prize, whereby it is “more likely than not” that the economic resources will flow 
out of the institution; and 
b. the institution is able to reliably measure the obligation  (including cost or expected cost  of 
in-kind promotional prizes) . 
8. An existing constructive obligation referred  to in paragraph 7(a), normally arises when  an 
announcement is clearly communicated  (through explicit statements or a consistent history of past 
actions) for the award of the promotional prize (s) to a specific class or classes of customers ( e.g., 
investment accountholders4), and resultantly, the institution has created a valid expectation among 
those parties that it will honour its commitment .  
9. The amount against provision for promotional prizes recognised  shall be charged as an expense , 
except where the requirements of paragraphs 15 apply . 
Subsequent measurement  
10. The carrying amount of the provision shall be reviewed at the end of each financial reporting period, 
and necessary adjustments shall be made. Any changes in the amount of provision shall be 
accounted for as a change in accounting estimate and recognised as an expense (or reversal thereof) 
for the period , except where the requirements of paragraph 15 apply.

 Loyalty program s 
Initial recognition  
11. An institution shall record a provision against obligation for loyalty programs based on the points 
accumulated to the customers, in line with the requirements of relevant AAOIFI FASs5 also 
considering the following , where significant : 
a. probability of expected utilisation of loyalty points by the customers; and 
b. timing of utilisation of loyalty points by the customers . 
12. The amount against provision for loyalty points recognised shall be charged as an expense.  
Subsequent measurement  
13. The carrying amount of the provision  shall be reviewed at the end of each financial reporting period, 
and necessary adjustments shall be made. Any changes in the amount of provision shall be 
accounted for  as a change in accounting estimate and recognised as  an expense (or reversal thereof) 
for the period.  
Classification and charging of expense s 
14. The expense  referred  to in paragraph s 6, 9-10 and 12-13, shall be recognised as promotional and 
marketing expense s. [Explanation: The promotional and marketing expenses shall be generally 
charged to the owners’ equity of the institution, unless the promotional gifts, promotional prizes 
and / or loyalty program relate to certain  asset -side or service -based products and the source of 
funding (and attribution of respective revenue) of such products is an investment pool, in which case 
it shall be considered as an expense of the investment pool .]  
15. Generally , the expenses in respect of promotional prizes shall be immediately recognised . However, 
there may be certain material  promotional  prizes that can justify a recognition of  a corresponding  
asset to be amortised over a period of time, to the extent that awarding of such promotional prize is 
not vested, subject to the following conditions:  
a. the promotional prize is part of a marketing and promotion campaign spanning over a 
period of at least 12 months whereby the promotional prize(s) is awarded at the end o f, or 
over the span of,  such  a period;  
b. the total obligation in respect of promotional prize(s) is considered material; and  
c. a positive correlation is demonstrated between  increased business (and hence, increased 
revenues  are expected ) against such asset to justify the matching concept through 
appropriate projections and financial models, provided that suitability of such projections / 
financial models shall be re -assessed at each financial reporting date.

 Presentation and disclosures  
16. In addition  to the presentation and disclosure requirements stated in FAS 1 "General Presentation 
and Disclosures in the Financial Statements", an institution shall disclose the following:  
a. a brief desc ription of significant  scheme(s) of  promotional  gifts, promotional prize s and 
loyalty programs , as applicable ; 
b. the accounting policies relating to promotional gifts , promotional prizes  and loyalty 
programs, as applicable ; and 
c. the provision and movement of provision, as well as, corresponding expenses in respect of 
promotional gifts, promotional prizes and loyalty programs , and non -vested provisions not 
yet expensed out . 
Effective date  
17. This standard shall be effective on the financial statements for the annual financial reporting period 
beginning on or after 01 January 2026 . Early adoption of the standard is  encouraged .

 Appendix C: Brief history of the preparation of the standard   
H1 AAOIFI Accounting Board ( AAB  / the board)  held its 31st meeting on 1-2 Jumad a I 1444H, 
corresponding to  25-26 November  2022 and decided  to develop a separate guidance note or 
standard for accounting of promotional gifts and prizes . 
H2 The working group  on revision of FAS 2 7, in its 9th meeting held on 2 Rabi ’ II 1445H corresponding to 
17 October  2023, discussed the draft  of FAS  on “Promotional Gifts and Prizes” . 
H3 AAB held its 3 5th meeting on 8-9 Rabi ’ II 1445H, corresponding to  23-24 October 2023 . It deliberated 
and principally approved the exposure draft for issuance.  
H4 The exposure draft was issued on 28 Jumada I 144 5H, corresponding to  12 December 2023.  
H5 A total of four public hearings (three in English language and one in Arabic language) for the 
exposure draft were held during  the period between April  2024  and August 2024.  
H6 AAOIFI Public Interest Monitoring Consultative Committee (PIMCC) held its 8th meeting on 8 Safar 
1446H, corresponding to  12 August 2024.  
H7 The working group in its 10th meeting held on 2 Safar 1446H, corresponding to  6 August 2024 
discussed all comments received from public hearings along with the views and comments from 
different industry participants on the exposure draft. After making necessary changes, the standard 
was recommended to the board for final approval.  
H8 AAB held its 39th meeting on 15 -16 Safar 1446H, corresponding to  19-20 August 2024. The board 
deliberated on changes suggested by the working group  along with comments from PIMCC  and the 
standard was approved in principle  for final issuance.  
H9 The committee of the Shari’ah board for review of accounting and governance standards held its 
43rd meeting on 6 Rabai’ II 1446H, corresponding to  9 October 2024 where the exposure draft was 
discussed duly taking the views for conformity with AAOIFI Shari’ah Standards.  
H10 After completion of the due process, the standard was issued on  8 Jumada I I 1446H, corresponding 
to 9 December 2024.
