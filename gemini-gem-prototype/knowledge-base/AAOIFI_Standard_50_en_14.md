 Preface  
PR1 Islamic investment institutions (IIIs) , including Islamic investment funds and other types of Shari’ah 
compliant investment entities , are a significant part of Islamic finance ecosystem, particularly the 
Islamic capital markets . Over time , IIIs have significantly evolved in their respective structure and 
size, which has emanated the need for improved  financial reporting guidelines .  
PR2 AAOIFI Accounting Bo ard (AAB / the board) decided to revise the existing AAOIFI Financial 
Accounting Standard (FAS) 14 “ Investment Funds” and to align it with other newer AAOIFI FASs  and 
pronouncement , specifically FAS  1 “General Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial 
Statements” and the “AAOIFI Conceptual Framework for Financial Reporting” . 
PR3 This standard supersedes FAS 14 “Investment Funds”.

 Introduction  
Overview  
IN1 This standard outlines  financial reporting principles applicable to the Islamic investment institutions  
(IIIs) . In particular, it emphasises on bringing harmony and standardisation with regard to the form 
and contents of the financial statements of IIIs. This standard prescribes the overall requirements for 
the presentation, minimum contents and recommended structure of their financial statements in a 
manner that facilitates truthful and fair presentation in line with Shari’ah principles and rules.   
Rationale for issuing  this standar d  
IN2 AAOIFI Accounting Board (AAB / t he board ), while concluding the Financial Accounting Standards 
(FAS) review and revision project  in 2018 , added the revision of FAS 14 “Investment Funds” to the 
agenda. The proposed revised standard was particularly aimed towards  aligning the financial 
reporting requirements for IIIs with the revised FAS 1 “General Presentation and Disclosures in the 
Financial Statements” and the “AAOIFI Conceptual Framework for Financial Reporting” (the 
conceptual framework).   
IN3 The board also agreed on the need for determining a wider scope which would include other types 
of IIIs in addition to investment funds  and to address certain pressing issues in respect of investment 
funds / investment entities accounting .  
Signi ficant changes from the previous standard  
IN4 This standard brings significant changes from its predecessor standard (FAS 14  “Investment Funds”), 
inter -alia, in the following aspects:  
a. the scope of the standard has been expanded to include different types of IIIs in addition to 
Islamic investment funds;  
b. the financial reporting principles have been aligned with the requirements of the revised FAS 
1 “General Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements” and the “AAOIFI 
Conceptual Framework for Financial Reporting” (the conceptual framework) , whereby this 
standard is to be read in conjunction with “Part 1: Applicable on all institutions” of FAS 1 
“General Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements” . In particular, the 
following changes have been introduced:  
i. definitions and terminologies used have been modified , cross -referred  and 
improved ; 
ii. certain presentation requirements e.g., the statement of portfolio investments, 
receivables and financing  have been aligned with the approach followed in newer 
AAOIFI FASs and hence, t his information is expected to be included in the notes to 
the financial statements which implies that a separate statement is no more 
required;  
iii. the concept of cash equivalent value , for recognition and measurement purposes,  
has been  removed ; and

 iv. the concept and the financial reporting principles for quasi -equity have been 
introduced whereby considering the type of III, as well as, the contractual 
arrangements with the investors, it has been clarified that their investments may 
take the form of either equity or quasi -equity;  
c. the financial reporting principles in respect of IIIs comprising of more than one virtual entity 
or sub -funds have been prescribed ;  
d. the concept and accounting requirements in respect of net asset value ( NAV ) differential are 
introduced to address the frequent entr ies to / exits from the open -ended  IIIs by the 
investor s; 
e. the predecessor  standard  included certain governance requirements and additionally certain 
requirements related to reporting to the stakeholders which are typically not a part of 
accounting  and financial reporting requirements. Such requirements have not been included 
in the revised standard;  
f. the predecessor standard, like certain other earlier AAOIFI FASs, included an illustrative 
format for financial statements . In line with the new policy of the board,  the illustrative 
financial statements  are not included in the revised standard, and are expected to be issued 
separately  in form of a technical release in due course;  and 
g. this standard clarifies that an III, due to its unique business model and intention  of 
investments , shall be considered exempt from following the equity accounting method or 
from consolidating an investee entity  into its financial statements .

Objective of the standard  
1. The objective of this standard is to set out the principles of financial reporting for Islamic investment 
institutions  (IIIs) particul arly prescribing overall  requirements for the presentation , minimum 
contents and recommended structure of their financial statements in a manner that facilitates 
truthful and fair presentation in line with Shari’ah principles and rules.   
Scope  
2. This standard shall apply to all Islamic investment institutions  (IIIs) . 
3. This standard shall also apply to employee retirement benefit funds managed by the Islamic financial 
institutions (IFIs) for the benefit of their own employees, in addition to the requirements of 
generally accepted accounting principles1. [Explanation: In particular, the disclosure  requirements  in 
respect of the nature of underlying assets and their respective compliance with Shari’ah principles 
and rules  shall be applicable .] 
4. This standard shall not be applicable on:  
a. financial repor ting for Sukuk -holders and the financial reporting of Sukuk  in the books of the 
originator , even if the underlying assets of the Sukuk are in the nature of pool of 
investment s, which are subject to the financial reporting requirements as prescribed in the 
relevant AAOIFI FAS s2;  
b. off-balance -sheet assets under management that do not take  the form of a separate legal 
entity  and which are subject to the financial reporting requirements as prescribed in the 
respective AAOIFI FAS3; 
c. investment institutions taking the form of a Waqf from Shari’ah perspective, which are 
subject to the financial reporting requirements as prescribed in the respective AAOIFI FAS4; 
and 
d. investment funds (e.g., participants’ investment funds) managed by Takaful institutions 
which are subject to the financial reporting requirements as prescribed in the relevant 
AAOIFI FASs5.

Definitions  
7. For the purpose of interpreting and applying this standard, the following short definitions are 
relevant:  
a. Back -end discount  – is the discount , on redemption of investment by  an outgoing investor , 
against net asset value (NAV) of such investment  applied in case of an III (being an open -
ended III) which is attributable to the equity or quasi -equity, as applicable ; 
b. Back -end fee – is the fee payable to the investment manager, as charged to an outgoing 
investor, normally computed on the basis of NAV of such investment in case of an III (being 
an open -ended III);  
c. Capital transactions – include the inflows  (e.g., through issuance of shares / units of 
investment (including reinvestment of distributable profits)) and outflow s (e.g., through 
redemption of units) of investment by investor s to increase or reduce their respective share 
in the net assets of an III  and may , at times,  include other directly made adjustments to 
equity / quasi -equity, e.g., reduction of share capital  against accumulated losses ; 
d. Fron t-end fee – is the fee payable to the investment manager , as charged to an incoming  
investor,  normally computed on the basis of NAV of such investment in case of an III (being 
an open -ended III);   
e. Front -end premium  – is the premium, if any, on amount of investment charged to an 
incoming investor,  against NAV of such investment applied in case of an III (being an open -
ended III)  which is attributable to the equity or quasi -equity, as applicable ; 
f. Investment manager  – is an institution which establishes, manages, operates and 
administers an III in a fiduciary capacity6, either in exchange for fee (fixed and / or variable) 
under an Al -Wakala Bi Al -Istithmar arrangement or against a share of profit (at times with 
incentives) under a Mudaraba arrangement based on profits or profit targets. [Explanation: 
An investment manager , in certain situations, can be an III itself];

 g. Investors – include  the participants in equity or quasi -equity of an III ; 
h. Islamic investment institutions (IIIs)  – are such Islamic financial institutions  (IFIs) , taking the 
juristic form of corporate entities, trusts and other financially and legally independent 
institutions, which:  
i. are established with an investment motive, i.e., to invest in a pool of Shari’ah 
compliant assets and earn returns on investments, without an expressed intention 
to exercise control  over the businesses underlying such investments;  and 
ii. involve investor(s) contributing to the equity or quasi -equity  of the III , and at times a 
combination thereof, which represent ownership claims to the underlying assets 
and entitlement to respective shares in profits or losses ; 
[Explanation: IIIs include open -ended and close -ended mutual funds, voluntary pension 
schemes, real estate investment trusts / funds, venture capital  institutions /  funds, private 
equity  institu tions / funds, sovereign wealth funds, family office investment institutions / 
funds, etc. , operating in line with the Shari’ah principles and rules ]; 
i. Issued value of shares / units – is the cumulative value against which shares / units of an III 
are subscribed by the investors against cash or through reinvestment of distributable profits , 
exclud ing NAV differential ; 
j. Net asset value ( NAV ) differential  – in the specific context of such open -ended IIIs  that 
maintain a nominal value of units ( as statutory capital ), is an account being a component of 
quasi -equity, used to record element of undistributed comprehensive income / loss included 
in net asset value of units as issued during the period less element of undistributed 
comprehensive income / loss included in the redemption value of units as redeemed during 
the period;   
k. Net asset value (NAV)  per share / unit – is the amount of net assets attributable to each 
share / unit at end of the financial reporting period. NAV  per share / unit shall be calculated 
by dividing net assets by the number of outstanding shares / units;  
l. Open -ended III – in the specific context of this standard, is an III in which investor s 
contractually (and by virtue of regulatory requirements) have a right to execute capital 
transactions , particularly the right to redeem their investments , all the time, on an over the 
counter  (OTC) basis ; 
m. Quasi -equity – in the general context, is an element of the financial statements that 
represents participatory contributions received by an institution on a profit sharing or 
participation basis. It has:  
i. primary characteristics of equity , i.e., in case of loss (unless negligence / misconduct 
/ breach of contractual terms is proved), the institution is not liable to return the  
lost funds to the fund providers and the fund providers share the residual interest in 
the underlying assets or business;

 ii. certain characteristics of a liability , i.e., it has a maturity or a put option of

iii. certain specific features , i.e., the rights of the fund providers are limited only to the

they do not have certain rights associated only with owners' equity ; 
[Explanation: In the specific conte xt of this standard, quasi -equity includes the units held by

one);

by AAOIFI insofar as these entail Shari’ah related requirements; and  
v. the approvals and rulings given by the Shari’ah supervisory board of the institution” ; 
and

 Assessment of business model and selection and adoption of accounting policies  
8. An III shall initially assess its business model, which shall form the basis for selection, adoption and 
application of accounting policies (and for applying significant judgments and estimates, where 
applicable).  Such accounting policies shall be adopted in line with the requirements of relevant 
AAOIFI FASs , unless contrary to a specific requirement of this standard.  
9. In assessment of business model, the expectations of the investors, particularly with regard to the 
investment returns, distributions of profits and withdrawals of quasi -equity, shall be considered  as 
prime  factors , along with the investment motives , investment objectives and risk appetite of the III.  
10. There is a rebuttable presumption that most IIIs have a business model that requires measurement 
of their investments, particularly investment in equity securities , on a fair value basis. However, any 
accounting policies to achieve the objective of fair valuation shall not be selected while 
compromising on the Shari’ah principles and rules or the requirements of the “AAOIFI Conceptual 
Framework for Financial Reporting” (the conceptual framework) . [Example: If an III enter s into a 
Murabaha transaction , it sh all follow the requirements of respective AAOIFI FAS8, instead of applying 
fair value accounting and, hence , record receivables and revenues in accordance with the 
requirements of respective AAOIFI FAS, which prohibits carrying Murabaha receivables at fair value .] 
11. In certain circumstances, where an open -ended III is required, or has a practice, to publish NAV per 
unit on a frequent basis, earlier than the end of the annual financial reporting period or respective 
interim financial reporting period, such an III shall apply the requirements related to interim 
financial reporting as per respective AAOIFI FAS9, insofar as practicable.

 Appendix B: Basis for c onclusions   
Scope  
Exclusion of investment funds not taking form of a legal entity from the scope  
BC1 AAOIFI Accounting Board ( the board / AAB) deliberated on the scope of the standard with regard to 
the inclusion , or otherwise , of investment  funds which do not take form of a legal entity / artificial 
juristic person. It was noted that t he earlier FAS 14 “Investment Funds” was also applicable to funds 
which might not be legal entities.  
BC2 It was  also noted  that, at times, it creates a confusion and overlapping of scope with regard to the 
investment funds and instruments which are part of the off -balance -sheet assets  under 
management . Moreover, certain members were of the view that the comprehensive financial 
reporting requirements to such investment funds which are not a legal entity would not be 
necessary  and would not justify the value addition as compared to the cost and efforts involved . 
The board further considered the concepts of contr ol as envisaged in the newer AAOIFI FASs (and 
the “AAOIFI Conceptual Framework for Financial Reporting”  (the conceptual framework )), and its 
impact on the determination of the scope for this standard.  
BC3 After due deliberations, the board decided that the new standard shall only be applicable to legal 
entities taking any juristic form.  
Expansion in the scope  
BC4 The earlier standard was only applicable to investment funds in which various investors contributed 
to the capital / equity of the fund. Its scope  did not necessarily include c ertain institutions  formed 
with investment motive , including certain investment funds formed by the government s or by 
individuals or a family, or any other form of a close group of investors (even enjoying a legal entity 
status).  
BC5 The board deliberated at length as to whether the scope of the new standard shall be determined 
based on primarily the “investment motive” , and hence includ e all investment institutions 
(including investment funds) or based on the earlier approach  apply to the investment funds  only . It 
was also considered as to whether it would be appropriate to keep other institutions / entities 
having an investment motive where various investors did not exist . The board further considered 
that in case the scope of this st andard remains limited to the investment funds, would it be needed 
to develop a separate standard for other investment institutions, and if so, would there be enough 
differences in the financial reporting requirements that justify having a separate standard.  
BC6 The board concluded that it would be more appropriate to expand the scope of the standard 
appropriately to include all Islamic investment institutions  (IIIs) . Such change in scope was also 
reflected in the name of the new standard.   
Employees’ retirement benefit funds  
BC7 The board discussed as to whether the employee benefit funds managed by the Islamic financial 
institutions  (IFIs)  for the benefit of, and on behalf of, their own employees shall be included in the 
scope of this standard.  After due consideration , the board decided that this standard shall also 
apply to employee retirement benefit funds managed by the IFI s, in addition to the requirements of 

## Page 20

 
20 
 generally accepted accounting principles  (subject to compliance with the Shari’ah principles and 
rules and the conceptual framework) . 
BC8 The board’s conclusion was based on the grounds  that, technically,  such funds may fall within the 
definition of IIIs, although they have a unique business model.  In addition, the board considered 
that there is a risk that if these are excluded from the scope of this standard and are subjected only 
to the generally accepted accounting principles, the accounting treatment of the underlying assets 
may not be in compl iance with Shari’ah principles and rules, which would not be considered 
appropriate .  
Investment managers / asset managers  
BC9 The board discussed if an institution in the nature of an investment manager or an asset manager 
classifies as an III, and hence shall be subject to the requirements of this standard. It was concluded 
that this standard shall apply on  an investment manager institution, if , and only if, it meets the 
definition of an III, but not by virtue of merely managing an III (including investment fund). 
However, the board decided that a specific exclusion from scope in this respect might also not be 
justified as there might be certain IIIs that also manage investment funds (or at times, sub -funds) .  
BC10  The board further considered, and concluded, that the activity of establish ing, manag ing, operat ing 
and administer ing an III in a fiduciary capacity, either in exchange of fee (fixed and / or variable) , 
e.g., under an Al -Wakala Bi Al -Istithmar arrangement or against a share of profit (at times with 
incentives) , e.g., under a Mudaraba arrangement in most situations may not constitute  control .  
BC11  However, where the investment manager also invests (quanti tatively material) in its managed  
investment fund based on Mudaraba structure, (taking the form of commingled Mudaraba  
investments ), it is probable that it may control such investments  or IIIs , and subject to meeting 
other relevant criteria, it may be classified as an III, and hence subject to the scope of this standard.  
Corporate restructuring entities  
BC12  The board discussed if the standard shall apply to corporate restructuring entities , which acquire a 
business in distressed condition for improvement and exit at a better price.  
BC13  The members of the board discussed that in respect of a corporate restructuring entity , while still 
meeting certain criteria of an III, a restructuring effort is involved . Such effort requires exercising of 
control over that investee (for improvement, revamping, restructuring purposes) . That effort, and 
exercise of control, changes the motive of the investor from the primary investment motive, which 
is a fundamental for an III.  Accordingly, the board decided not to include the corporate 
restructuring companies within the definition of III or the scope of the standard and left it to be 
decided according to the business model.  
BC14  With regard to the matter of exemption to the corporate restructuring entities available under 
generally accepted accounting principles from consolidation or equity method accounting  (in form 
of exemption for acquisition of subsidiaries / investment in associate s for disposal in short -term), it 
was decided that this matter shall be dealt in the project related to revision of AAOIFI FASs related 
to consolidation and investment in associates.

 Alignment, and avoidance of duplication, with the requirements  of FAS 1 “General 
Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements”   
BC15  The board discussed the need for alignment of the requirements of this standard with the 
requirements of part 1 of FAS 1 “General Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements” 
and deliberated as to whether the standard shall be a comprehensive integrated solution or shall be 
developed as a complimentary standard to be read in conjunction with the same.  
BC16  Certain members of the board and the working group were of the view that a single comprehensive 
standard is more convenient for adoption and implementation purposes and hence, preferred the 
same.  
BC17  Other members were of the opinion that the standard could compliment the requirements of part 1 
of FAS 1 “General Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements” , which, as its name 
implies, applies to all institutions and consequently is also applicable to IIIs. Accordingly, the 
requirements of both standards shall consequently not overlap  and avoid unnecessary duplication.  
This approach is similar to that of generally accepted accounting principles, where standards 
addressing specific topics are  mostly not comprehensive and independent in nature.  
BC18  The board , after due deliberations,  decided that this standard shall be developed as a 
complementary standard addressing the unique aspects of IIIs and their business model only, while 
all definitions  and basic financial reporting requirements particularly those related to the 
presentation and disclosures shall be read in conjunction with part 1 of FAS 1  “General Presentation 
and Disclosures in the Financial Statements” . 
BC19  The conclusion of the board was based on the premise that the scope of FAS 1 “General 
Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements” has been enhanced to include all 
institutions, whether offering banking services or otherwise, and hence it is not appropriate to 
duplicate the presentation and disclosure requirements that are already available in part 1 of FAS 1 
“General Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements” in this standard. This approach 
also avoids inconsistencies among vari ous standards that may arise as a result of replicating 
requirements. Furthermore, it provides ease in incorporating any current and future amendments 
in other standards that may have an effect on this standard. This practice is further consistent with 
generally accepted accounting principles, earlier AAOIFI standards  and AAOIFI technical releases , as 
well.  
Selection of accounting policies in respect of  recognition and measurement   
BC20  The earlier FAS 14 prescribed the basis of valuation to be used on initial recognition and subsequent 
measurement of investments, receivables and financing. It also dealt with valuation of real estate 
and certain other assets. In certain circumstances, duly considering the unique business model of 
investment funds, such recognition, measurement and valuation practices were different from  
those  defined  in other relevant AAOIFI FASs .  
BC21  The board discussed the need to provide such  requirements in the standard  in addition to the 
provisions of relevant AAOIFI FASs . The members of the board also deliberated upon the valuation 
of certain assets where specific guidelines are already provided in the relevant AAOIFI FASs and / or 
generally accept ed accounting principles . In particular, i t was also discussed as to whether it is more 

## Page 22

 
22 
 appropriate that an III shall apply only the fair value accounting for measurement of all underlying 
assets  or the application of AAOIFI  FAS’s hierarchy for selection of accounting policies  would be 
better ? 
BC22  After considering multiple factors  and relevant arguments , the board concluded that the III s shall  
select and apply accounting policies according to the relevant hierarchy provided by the AAOIFI 
FASs , while taking into account their respective business model . The board considered that in 
general, it may be presumed that most, but not all, IIIs’ business model would require them to keep 
the recognition and measurement requirements based on fair values of the underlying ass ets.  
BC23  The board’s decision, inter -alia, was based on the premise that the matters pertaining to 
recognition and measurement are specialised subjects and have been addressed in multiple 
relevant AAOIFI FASs  and hence any deviation from such standardi sed practices would result in 
different accounting practices within the Islamic finance industry. The board also considered that 
such standards allow the flexibility required according to the business model and hence there is no 
need to develop  an entirely separate framework . The board further concluded that this approach 
shall avoid any current and future inconsistencies with other standards.  
BC24  With regard to the fair valuation to be the primary basis for recognition and measurement of all 
underlying assets, the board did not agree with the proposal on the grounds that, firstly, not all 
underlying assets may be reported at fair value in line with the Shari’ah principles and rules (and 
the conceptual framewo rk), e.g., the established receivables , and secondly, because  the business 
model of the IIIs may not justify the use of fair values in certain cases.  
Primary f inancial statements of an III   
BC25  The earlier FAS 14 c ontained an option for an investment fund to present either a statement of 
changes in net assets or a statement of cash flows.  
BC26  The board deliberated on the need to retain the simplified option of presenting a statement of 
changes in net assets (which would result in not presenting a statement of changes in equity and a 
statement of cash flows) or keep  the complete set of financial statements  in line with the 
requirements of FAS 1 “General Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements”  and 
generally accepted accounting principles .  
BC27  After due perusal, the board concluded that the option of presenting a statement of changes of net 
assets  (and hence, not presenting a statement of cash flows and statement of changes in equity)  
should not be retained in the new standard. The board ’s conclusion was based on the premises that 
this would enable and encourage harmonisation in the financial statements of III s and that a 
statement of changes in net assets might suit certain investment funds but not all types of IIIs being 
subject to the expanded  scope of this standard.  
Investment institution / entity exemption from consolidation  
BC28  The generally accepted accounting principles provide an exemption from consolidation to an 
investment entity , which is apparently not consistent with the relevant AAOIFI FASs , currently . 
AAOIFI secretariat had received, in the recent past, certain queries from the market players related 
to this inconsistency , which was reported to the board in due course. Since this standard specifically 

## Page 23

 
23 
 addresses the IIIs, the board considered the need to incorporate an exemption from consolidating 
an investee entity / institution.  
BC29  Certain members of the board were of the view that when an entity qualifies as an III and applies 
this standard, it is neither required to consolidate with the financial statements of the investee 
company nor does it need to follow the equity method of acc ounting. The III may either establish or 
acquire a shareholding subsequently in an investee company as its normal course of business. Such 
interest in shareholding is not primarily intended to exercise influence or control over the investee 
company  or to get involved in its business but  is primarily to generate investment returns for its 
own investors .  
BC30  The board agreed that the motive or objective of the investment shall play an important role in 
determining the application of the exemption from consolidation. Once it is established that the 
objective behind investment is only to gain returns from investment (including returns attribution 
to its own investors, in particular) , the III shall not consolidate , or follow the equity method of 
accounting in respect of, its investee companies .  
BC31  After due deliberations,  the board decided that , generally, the business model of an III does not 
allow or require preparation of consolidated financial statements. Accordingly,  the board concluded 
that t his standard shall apply only to the stand -alone financial statements of IIIs. An III shall not 
prepare consolidated financial statements in respect of any entity that it controls, nor shall it apply 
the equity method of accounting in respect of any entity  over which it exercises significant 
influence.  
BC32  However, the board further considered and concluded that t he existence of control or exercise of 
significant influence by an III over another entity may, at times, require a re -assessment of the 
business model of the III, and hence applicability of this standard on such III.  
Governance requirements  
BC33  The earlier FAS 14 contained certain requirements related to governance of investment funds and 
disclosures related to those requirements  including , e.g., the Shari’ah board’s report  and the 
statement of financial highlights .  
BC34  The board concluded that the  governance and performance  indicators related requirements which 
are not part of the financial reporting requirements shall not be included in this standard , as this is 
primarily a financial reporting standard. However, duly considering  the significance of qualitative 
aspects of financial reportin g, a disclosure about the governance framework being followed by the 
III in line with the requirements of paragraph 56 (g) of FAS 1 “General Presentation and Disclosures 
in the Financia l Statements” shall be mandatory .

Allowing “statement of net assets ” as a primary statement instead of “statement of 
financial position ”  
BC36  The exposure draft of the standard allowed using statement of net assets as a primary statement 
instead of statement of financial position which is required by “AAOIFI  Conceptual Framework for 
Financial Reporting ” and FAS 1 “ General Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements ”. 
Certain observations were made on this choice and hence the working group and the board

statement of net assets for IIIs, particu larly for Islamic investment funds.  
BC37  The board noted that there is no fundamental difference between the two statements . However, in 
the context of not -for-profit entities and investment pools, the market practices are tilted toward 
use of statement of net assets, particularly where there is no share capital but rather different 
type s of equity, quasi -equity and funds, etc . In addition, certain regulatory and supervisory 
authorities (RSAs) also require a statement of net assets in case of Islamic investment funds.   
BC38  Accordingly, the board re affirmed  its view  of allowing use of either of the two statements as 
originally proposed in the exposure draft  of the standard .

 Appendix C: Brief history of the preparation of the standard  
H1 The 1st working group meeting for the revision of AAOIFI Financial Accounting Standard (FAS) 14 
“Investment Funds” was held on 18 Muharram 1442H, corresponding to 6 September 2020 , where 
the preliminary study was presented and discussed.  The working group was convened as a result of 
the decision of the AAOIFI Accounting Board (AAB  / the board ) to revise the existing standard in light 
of the standards review and revision project.  
H2 The board, in its 26th meeting held on 28-29 Rabi ’ I 1443H, corresponding to 3-4 December 2021, 
emphasised on the need to prioritise the revision of the standard, which had been postponed due to 
the finalisation of the “AAOIFI Conceptual Framework for Financial Reporting ” and FAS 1 “General 
Presentation and Disclosures in the Financial Statements” , and before the completion of which the 
revision of the standard would not have been appropriate . 
H3 The working group held its 2nd meeting on 8 Safar 1445H, corresponding to 24 August 2023, where 
the exposure draft of the  revised  standard was presented and deliberated upon by the members. 
The comments received from the working group members were accordingly  incorporated . 
H4 The exposure draft of the standard was presented to the board in its 35th meeting, held on 8-9 Rabi ’ 
II 1445H , corresponding to  23-24 October 2023 , where by after detailed discussion, it was approved 
in principle . After completion of due process, the exposure draft of the standard was issued on 22 
Jumada I 1445H , corresponding to 6 December 2023 , inviting public comments  thereon . 
H5 The public hearings on the exposure draft of the standard were conducted between Muharram to 
Rabi ’ II 1446H, corresponding to July to October 2024 , in both English and Arabic  languages , through 
video  conferencing.  
H6 All comments received from the public hearings along with the views and comments received in 
writing from different industry participants on the exposure draft of the standard were presented 
for discussion at the 3rd working group meeting held on 24 Rabi ’ II 1446H , corresponding to 27 
October 2024 . After due deliberation, the draft standard was forwarded to the board for its perusal 
and final approval.  
H7 The draft standard  was presented to the board at its 41st meeting convened on 30 Rabi ’ II 1446H , 
corresponding to 2 November 2024 . In this meeting, the standard was approved in principle.  
H8 The Shari’ah committee17, in its 45th meeting held on 27 Jumada I 1446H, corresponding to 28 
November 2024, duly discussed the standard confirming its conformity with Shari’ah  principles and 
rules . 
H9 After completing the due process and incorporating comments from the board, the standard was 
issued on 23 Jumada II 1446H, corresponding to 24 December 2024.
